# Malaria Trends in Africa: A Python Data Analysis

## Project Overview
This project analyzes malaria incidence trends across Africa using data from the World Health Organization (WHO). The analysis explores changes in malaria incidence between 2000 and 2024, identifies countries with the highest malaria burden, compares trends among selected East African countries, and highlights years with the greatest improvements.

This project demonstrates the use of Python for data cleaning, exploratory data analysis, visualization, and generating public health insights.

## Objectives
The project answers the following questions:
1. How many African countries are represented?
2. What period does the dataset cover?
3. What is the distribution of malaria incidence?
4. Which African countries have the highest malaria incidence?
5. How has malaria incidence changed over time?
6. Which African countries achieved the largest reduction in malaria incidence?
7. How do malaria incidence trends in Kenya compare with those of Uganda, Tanzania, Rwanda, and Ethiopia?
8. Which years showed the greatest improvement in malaria incidence?

# Conclusions
Based on the analysis, malaria incidence in Africa has generally declined between 2000 and 2024, indicating progress in malaria control efforts. However, the burden remains unevenly distributed, with West and Central African countries continuing to record the highest incidence rates. Several countries, including Guinea-Bissau and Burkina Faso, achieved substantial reductions over the study period, demonstrating that sustained interventions can significantly reduce malaria incidence. Kenya performed relatively well compared to its East African neighbors, while Uganda continued to report the highest incidence.

# Recommendations
1. Strengthen malaria prevention and treatment programs in high-burden countries, particularly in West and Central Africa.
2. Continue investing in proven interventions such as insecticide-treated mosquito nets, indoor residual spraying, and early diagnosis.
3. Share successful malaria control strategies from countries that achieved large reductions with countries still experiencing high incidence.
4. Maintain continuous surveillance to quickly detect increases in malaria cases and respond effectively.
5. Ensure malaria control programs remain resilient during public health emergencies such as pandemics.
